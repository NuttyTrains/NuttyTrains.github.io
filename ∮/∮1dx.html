I am not going to step on James' toes here, given that this is the largest chunk of text James has ever written, or will ever write on this website. So I am briefly going to expand upon my (now-limited) memories of this adventure, 6 months after it happened.

This adventure was born from the ashes of NT Epsilon, which was cancelled thanks to Boris. This adventure would have involved Matthew catching a dirt-cheap early morning train to Exeter (SWR *vomits*), before we slowly headed east to the Isle of Wight. We cancelled all our tickets, afraid of chockdown 3, but with only days to go, with chockdown 3 not happening (yet), Matthew still had his dirt-cheap ticket to Exeter. Matthew got what he paid for. The train was miserable, slow and had no sockets. It was dire. He was extremely glad when he arrived in Exeter St Davids, went to Starbucks, and sat there charging his laptop, waiting for James to turn up.

Matthew is always late. It simply wouldn't have done to be arriving on time. After all, James was in the driving seat this time, and when in Rome...

Exmouth Line

The first port of call was the Exmouth line. We reversed back up the line towards Salisbury, before turning towards the coast. We passed through a number of quiet stations, and the line ran alongside the muddy flats of the Exe floodplain. James gave Matthew an ornithology lesson, by teaching him the names of all the lines in the South West, all named after birds that Matthew had never heard of or seen before. Nor can he remember them, because this was 6 months ago. All he can remember is that we spotted some bird-like things by the river. It was a yummy grey December day, so the river and the sky looked beautiful.

As far as South West branch lines go, this was the easiest of the bunch, despite Matthew thinking it was extremely complicated. Once per hour, a train would stop at every station on the line, with only 2 request stops. Unfortunately, the latter of these, Lympstone Commando, was completely dead, with almost 0 passengers. James assumed this would be the main difficulty.

Fortunately, someone wanted to stop at Exton, the other request stop, so that left only Lympstone Commando. We asked the conductor if he could stop the train. As we pulled into the station, we were greeted by a horrible concrete and barbed wire monstrosity of an army base. It looked like Colditz in the grey mizzle. We both hopped off, and James started to walk towards the exit, fully intending to walk to Lympstone Village. Matthew took another look at Colditz, looked back at the train, jumped back on, and said "thank you" to the conductor, before sitting down, to James' horror. The audacity of that boy! The cheek! The nerve! Down Kernow, you'd never do that! That damn city boy!

We rocked up into "sunny", "pleasant" Exmouth - a vile port town surrounded by a grey pallid sea and beige mudflats. We fucked around for a little, Matthew walked backwards through a tunnel, we jumped over a fence, got bored, had our station photo, and returned to the train, this time heading to Torquay.
