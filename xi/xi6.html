Here we changed to Coventry, which is certainly a station of all time (we have both since visited Coventry - it is a city of all time). We took a train via Birmingham International and headed straight to Birmingham New St. As this was Birmingham, and we were both depressed that day, we did not appreciate this very much. We elected to immediately leave Birmingham and head south (once again, our glorious friend TB ignored this and sent us on a jolly to Crewe).

We took the line all the way south to Redditch, missing Bromsgrove entirely, before having 10 minutes in the station car park, too afraid to leave, lest we had to interact with the locals, before getting back on the train and returning up the line. Unfortunately, as we missed Bromsgrove, and because they are busily constructing a second line in this direction (via Pineapple Road), we will one day have to return here. This is much to Matthew's chagrin, as he is a very poor PhD candidate (relative to James), who now is the one who lives in the middle of nowhere... this means that a Birmingham trip necessitates a long and expensive trip, just to meet up in some dump. NT was much more fun when living in London. Still, at least we got to see Bournville (the site of the Cadbury factory).

Unfortunately, we had found ourselves back in Birmingham once more. Birmingham? On a Birmingham adventure? Disgusting. This would have to be corrected... what if we headed to Markeyland instead? For once, Matthew acquiesced - even Markeyland had to be better than Birmingham. Just look that that face: is that a face that is happy to be in Birmingham? (Is that a face that is happy to be alive??)

In no time at all, we were zipping out towards Wolverhampton - glorious Wolverhampton. I say 'zipped' - we were on a slow train. This just have us even more time to admire the post-industrial hellscape as we pulled into the city. Needless to say, we were very pleased when our actual fast train turned up, and we actually zipped almost straight to Shrewsbury... ON A MARKEY TRAIN!! It felt very good to be back on a Markey train. We love Markey very much. Our brain chips flooded our brains with oxytocin in increasing concentrations as we approached the Welsh border. Markey was welcoming us home!

We disembarked at Shrewsbury to have a look around the town, given that the last time we were here, we managed to see nothing but the multistorey car park and the Subway. This time we saw a little more - it was actually not so bad. Naturally, we spent most of the time putting on squeaky voices, to pay homage to another aspiring Ffynone videographer (I have forgotten his name - James: help plz).

But then, we made Markey sad. We made Markey angry. We would not be coming home. We were returning to Birmingham.

Apart from a hilarious clip where Matthew jammed his hand in the door twice (which is likely to be the only Xi 6 video that ever sees the light of day), this is where things became darker. I want to blame Markey's brain chip, but in reality, neither of us were well, and Matthew was especially unwell. This was the adventure where James realised exactly how unwell Matthew really was. Things were not good. The amnesia zone exists for a reason. The spiciest quotes will not be repeated here (I have wiped most from my memory), so instead, I leave you with some hilarious quotes and a gorgeous photo. Writing in 2024, I am very glad that we are both out of this now.
