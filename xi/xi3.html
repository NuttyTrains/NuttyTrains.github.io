This adventure actually starts the previous night, when James turned up drunk as a rat to Matthew's house in Kensal, bloody barely standing. He wandered in drunkenly, almost wanting to vomit after he had ingested twice the lethal dosage of ethanol at Usman's party in South Ken. Oh and his nails were painted. After spending 2 hours being brutally offensive and just generally drunk out his mind whilst talking to Matthew and his housemate Max, James eventually started to sober up, but only enough to play some art student bingo with Max, quote Borat back and forth with Max, and ridicule Matthew's stir fry, before falling asleep on Matthew's floor and starting to snore. With that, Matthew ushered James downstairs and into bed.

The next morning was rough. Rough as hell. Especially as the extremely hungover James was rudely awoken by Freya's alarm clock at 7am.

But you know who was having a rougher morning? Thameslink. Whilst we were planning our course of action in the morning, we noticed something strikingly familiar about James' visits to London - literally every service out towards Cambridge was completely fucking dead. Thameslink had offed itself.

Thameslink wasn't alone. As we arrived at Kensal Green tube station, the Bakerloo had offed itself too, so we ticked off the Overloo into Euston - tick! There goes our lovely Koppa-only TfL table! Who needs clean divisions anyway?

We walked to St Pancras and bought Greggs for breakfast, as James was dying of hunger and being hungover, laughing at the French people leaving the Eurostar. We then changed to Kings Cross and just caught the 1st operational Thameslink train of the day towards Cambridge, stopping at many interesting places. Unfortunately, we had no cameras for this section of the trip, so the video you will see for this adventure will be decidedly old school - PHONE TIME!

There are a few pretty stations on the way, with Potters Bar having its own Sainsbury's, Shepreth having its own zoo, and Welwyn Garden Tesco having its own station. The land became flatter and flatter.

We arrived in Cambridge at about 2, and headed straight for Ctaz, where James was staying. James then decided to choke on some hard Cambridge water, Matthew ate some plain white bread, and James' cameras had a few spasms as we watched our new video, which you should too if you are sad enough to read this.

We then set out to explore Cambridge, starting with Jacks Gelato, which must be laced with a few milliDruths of cwac, because James is addicted and can often be found pawing at the glass window of the shop at 11pm at night, waiting for his 10th ice cream of the day. From this moment on, forget the city, forget the uni, forget the colleges, and even forget Part III - the real reason James is applying to Cambridge is Jack's Gelato! You heard it here first.

We walked up out of the city centre, past Magdalen, and climbed all the way to the top of Castle Mound, where we admired the view for a bit, before filming some crap on the way down, including an excellent Tom Scott parody - SINGLE TAKE!

We passed the touristy street (St Johns, Trinity, Kings and the Corpus Christi clock), popped back into Catz briefly, then headed out to Selwyn to admire it for a bit (whilst also complaining about Grange Road). Hopefully James will be back in Cambridge when Ed Milliband eats a bacon sarnie live in front of an audience, so that we can watch some quality entertainment when we are there next!

We walked back up Silver Street, then doubled down Mill Lane towards the river to admire it in the twilight. It was very very pretty. We both liked it a lot. James also really liked seeing REDACTED and rubbing it in.

We then wandered around 3 sites in succession - Mill Lane, New Museums and Downing, as Matthew attempted to find a particular building that he liked (it was Downing site), and James straight up broke into a lecture hall. I bet that everyone was very impressed by that!

Finally, we reached Emmanuel College, and wandered around the perimeter to heal Matthew's battle scars, whilst James did the same at Christs by laughing at them loudly and publically. We skirted around the back of the bus station after finding that our chosen curry house was closed (RIP), avoided the giant queue at Zizzi's, and noticed Bella Italia was closed, so we went to Bryon Burger instead, and grossly abused the promise of "bottomless fries".

"Do you ever eat so much meat that you get the meat sweats?"

Finally, we paid and retuned to Catz to rest our weary legs and backs. After roughly an hour, we headed out once more towards the station, with James turning back about halfway. Matthew stole "borrowed" James' bike in a completely non-dodgy way (whacking the lock until it loosened), before boarding the train.

But yet again, the trains were fucked, so the conductor advised we gave up and went to Liverpool St instead... which is what Matthew did.
