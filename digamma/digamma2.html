We jumped on and off on this valley-type line as it diverged from the mainline and headed up into the hills of Clifton. We disembarked at Clifton Down and decided to head first to the zoo. That's right. We headed to the zoo. Nutty Trains goes to the zoo. Yep. Zoo time.

Zoo time was brief. This being Nutty Trains, we had no time to actually go to the zoo, no tickets, and nothing there to actually see, do or film, so we stood in the car park for 30 seconds, just enough time for James to talk some shit about the place and headed off to the real target: Clifton Gorge.

This can mainly be described as a mistake, cooked up the night before by Matthew. We threw ourselves down this steep forest path, almost slipping, falling and dying on more than one occasion, descending an entire cliff-face's drop down to the bottom of the gorge. We crossed the road (remember that bit), and started to walk towards the suspension bridge. Truth be told, it was a magnificent walk. It may have been next to a loud and smelly road, and a foul and turbulent river, but the gorge and the bridge overhead were beautiful and striking. When the clips from this adventure finally see the light of day when James gets internet in 2027, they are going to be spectacular. The stations became more infrequent as we left the city, with the times between stations roughly doubling. The allotments didn't give up though - those remained in full force all the way to Severn Beach. Well, almost all the way. See, when we missed our original train, it turns out that we had also missed the train that stopped at all the stations. We stood by the door after we left Avonmouth, and as the train wound its way through the world's most miserable incineration plant, eagerly awaiting the train to slow down and stop at St Andrews Road. Alas, it was not to be. The train flew through the station at its maximum speed, so we watched the station sign fly past the doors at 10 miles per hour. RIP St Andrews Road. Matthew was deeply upset. Now we'd have to come back to this shithole and do this whole ordeal again!
