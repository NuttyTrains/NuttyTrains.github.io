Going to Bristol's most notorious and least used station with two trains a week, all while Bristols train network falls to bits around them? Honestly, I cannot think of a better idea!

Let's just start this account with the standard disclaimer that this was not planned well. Not at all. Why we chose this date, I do not know. Neither does James - he had already attempted to do this adventure solo the week before, which would have been a much smarter date to do this. Whilst Digamma 1.9 was mostly a write off, and James failed to reach Pilning, so ended up just going home, that was better than what we faced on Digamma 2: the entire rail network in Brizzle being a complete write-off. This was going to be fun!

As seems to be a tradition for adventures where Matthew has to travel across the country, Matthew had failed to sleep the night before, so was going to try and conduct this adventure fuelled entirely by iced coffee. He caught the train from Paddington and headed to Brizzle on the gay train (800008: everyone's favourite!). A completely uneventful, on-time journey. If only the same could be same for James...

So Matthew arrived in Brizzle and then had to wait for the next >2 hours for James to arrive. James' phone was almost as dead as Matthew was, so James requested that Matthew pick him up a charger. Thus, Matthew's first course of action was to head south from Temple Meads, cross the river, and head toward Bedminster. Once this task was complete, he crossed back across the Avon, and headed towards the old docks. Here, he stopped off at what had been secretly planned to be our first stop: a ship called The Matthew. This ship was sailed by John Cabot across the Atlantic when he discovered Newfoundland in 1497. Matthew said this all on camera for a video segment, but James may or may not be interested in 'accurate' information. Matthew also saw a sign including Stephen the seagull, which seemed very appropriate for NT.

Matthew then started to return to Temple Meads, via the outskirts of the city centre, and via Castle Park. He walked alongside the river and along the side of the huge road leading back to the station. Just as he was about to reach Temple Meads, he came across possibly the most traumatising sight he has ever seen...

A seagull eating a Meal Deal

It was awful. Terrible. Revolting. The seagulls dragged Meal Deal's poor and sorry carcass around, ripping chunks off and almost severing its head... all on camera for our valued viewers! It traumatised Matthew to film, and traumatised James to edit, so now it can traumatise you as you watch it. Enjoy!


After this horrific experience, Matthew called James only to find that James was still nowhere near arriving, so Matthew headed into the closest Starbucks, not for coffee (surprisingly), but to use the bathroom. He stood outside the door for a full 20 minutes, as the bathroom remained occupied, until a lady customer came to also use the bathroom, and taught Matthew the trick of just using a 2p coin to forcefully unlock the door from the outside, which was genius. Soon after this, Matthew returned to the ticket hall in Temple Meads, and awaited James' arrival. What was James getting up to in the meantime?
