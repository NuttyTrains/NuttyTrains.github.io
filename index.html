About Us

Trains

We are self pronounced train addicts. James, after a trip to London on a HST and only having been on a train once before, was hooked. He was happy to see the introduction of the IET Class 800s and sad to see the end of the Pacers. Before he knew it he was mourning the loss of Arriva Trains Wales, Ave Arriva! Matthew, it was after his many adventures off on the trains and the call of the valleys, it was accentuated by seeing trains everyday going past at college, soon enough, he knew the patterns and told the time by what train went by and questioned descrepencies in the train timetable. What could this project possibly be? Well some people challenge themselves to go to every station in the UK. Together a little before summer 2018 we set off to do that very thing. Every station in the UK, visited by us in some time in the future! We set ourselves the following rules:

  1. To say we visited a station we must:
    • Be on a train that stops at the station
    • Touch the platform
    • Take a photograph of the station sign
    • (Optional due to short platforms) Run between the carriage doors (James' Job)
  2. We are allowed to use public transport (other than trains), to arrive or leave a station, but not both. We must arrive or leave on a train.
  3. We are allowed to use parlimentary trains and heritage railways, aslong as we validly tick off the station.

Fast forward a few months, and James is busy pottering about on his Cornish valley lines (it's a pity trains don't stop at his home station on a Wednesday), meanwhile Matthew is zipping about on the Tube every day, and actually lives next to the Central line. As a result, he has realised that the Tube and other metropolitan services function very differently from National Rail elsewhere, meaning a different set of rules is required:

  1. To say we visited a station we must:
    • Be on a train that stops at the station
    • Step on the platform as a minimum (although alighting is encouraged)
    • Take a picture of the station sign at any station where we alight
    • Tap out roughly once an hour, to avoid maximum journey times
  2. During peak hours, we must alight, switch carriages, and catch the next train
  3. We are allowed to use public transport (other than trains), to arrive or leave a station, but not both. We must arrive or leave on a train.
  4. We are allowed to use parlimentary trains and heritage railways within Greater London, as well as the Epping-Ongar Railway, provided we validly tick off the station.
  5. These rules apply to:
    • London Underground
    • London Overground
    • TfL Rail/Elizabeth Line
    • DLR
    • Tramlink
    • National Rail Services within the Oyster PAYG zone
    • Epping-Ongar Railway
  6. Our station table will be organised alphabetically, as opposed to numerically, to allow for the provision of multiple lines at each station
