I must note that we are NOT train spotters, but instead Railway enthusiasts

Well, you've found us. Welcome to the bastion of nuttiness that is Nutty Trains! You're probably going to regret finding us. We should probably introduce ourselves. Hello, we are James and Matthew, but seeing as you are here, you probably already knew that. Here we record all of our nutty adventures together. Think of this website as the diary of our alter-egos, hidden behind layers of stuffiness, awkwardness and maths. Our insane alter-egos. These are not normal adventures, where we have a jolly day out, perhaps by the sea, or to a cafe to have tea. No. Our adventures involve visiting random run-down places, excessive running, plenty of mockery and scorn, and trains. So many trains.

We feel as if we ought to stress that we are interested in the railways, but we are not trainspotters. Our adventures do not consist of standing by some track, watching trains for hours, noting down serial numbers. We aren't interested in that. Whilst we both find trains cool (years of Thomas the Tank Engine will do that to you), our fascination is with the railway, not just the trains. We are interested in the lines, the stations, the destinations, the network, the trains and the inevitable mistakes that we witness on our adventures. And there are lots of them!

James was hooked after a trip to London on a HST, having been on a train only once before. He was happy to see the introduction of the IET Class 800s and will be sad to see the end of the Pacers... if they ever leave. Before he knew it he was mourning the loss of Arriva Trains Wales. Ave Arriva! Matthew, having convinced James of the superiority of trains, had had many adventures on the trains and his enjoyed watching trains going past at college everyday, to the point that he could tell the time from watching the trains!

Soon, the call of the valleys became too great, and we set off on our first nutty train adventure together. Along the way, we decided to transform this one-off adventure into a ginormous project. What could this project possibly be? Well some people challenge themselves to go to every station in the UK. Together, in December 2018, we set off to do that very thing. We would visit every station in the UK, (at some point in the possibly distant future), according to our own set of particular rules!
We set ourselves the following rules:

  1. To say we visited a station we must:
    • Be on a train that stops at the station
    • Touch the platform
    • Take a photograph of the station sign
    • (Optional due to short platforms) Run between the carriage doors (James' Job)
  2. We are allowed to use public transport (other than trains), to arrive or leave a station, but not both. We must arrive or leave on a train.
  3. We are allowed to use parlimentary trains and heritage railways, aslong as we validly tick off the station.

Fast forward a few months, and James is busy pottering about on his Cornish valley lines (it's a pity trains don't stop at his home station on a Wednesday), meanwhile Matthew is zipping about on the Tube every day, and actually lives next to the Central line. As a result, he has realised that the Tube and other TfL services function very differently from National Rail elsewhere, meaning a different set of rules is required:

  1. To say we visited a station we must:
    • Be on a train that stops at the station
    • Step on the platform as a minimum (although alighting is encouraged)
    • Take a picture of the station sign at any station where we alight
    • Tap out roughly once an hour, to avoid maximum journey times
  2. During peak hours, we ought to alight, switch carriages, and catch the next train
  3. We are allowed to use public transport (other than trains), to arrive or leave a station, but not both. We must arrive or leave on a train.
  4. We are allowed to use any TfL service, with the addition of the Epping-Ongar Railway, provided we validly tick off the station.
  5. These rules apply to:
    • London Underground
    • London Overground
    • TfL Rail/Elizabeth Line
    • DLR
    • Tramlink
    • Epping-Ongar Railway
  6. Our station table will be organised alphabetically, as opposed to numerically, to allow for the provision of multiple lines at each station
  7. These rules do not apply to National Rail services in the Oyster PAYG zone. These rules apply only to TfL services.

After our amazing attempt at the #IKEAmeansIKEA challenge, we have decided to visit all the IKEAs in the UK. There are only 18 of them? How hard can it be?

  1. To get to an IKEA we must:
    • Use a train to arrive at the closest station to an IKEA
    • Follow all above NT rules at the station
    • Walk to the IKEA, no matter how impossible that may be
  2. To say we visited an IKEA we must:
    • Walk through all the showrooms - no shortcuts!
    • Visit the restaurant
    • Steal >1 pencil
    • Most importantly, we must purchase at least 1 item of random crap!
  3. We are allowed to leave in the manner in which we arrived, or take the bus. That doesn't matter
